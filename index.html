Hiring Committee

Dear hiring committee,

I'm excited to apply for the Director, Marketing and Communications role at Arts Umbrella.

For most of my career, I've had the privilege of working with organizations that are trying to make the world better. That matters to me. I do my best work when I can connect strategy, creativity and digital expertise to a mission I care about.

The arts are a meaningful part of my life. I'm actively involved in community theatre, and I've seen first-hand how the arts build confidence, discipline, empathy, imagination and a stronger sense of self. I also believe they are essential to a healthy society. A vibrant arts landscape does not happen by accident. It starts with access, participation and investment in young people, so more families can see the arts as something available to them, not something distant or exclusive.

That is what draws me to Arts Umbrella. The organization plays an important role in helping young people build creative lives, while also helping more families and communities understand the value of the arts.

My work has consistently focused on helping mission-driven organizations gain clarity, focus on priorities and build practical systems that make their work more effective while centring human storytelling. I bring a mix of brand strategy, marketing strategy, digital expertise and data-informed decision-making, with deep experience across websites, content, SEO/AEO, audience journeys and organizational strategy.

In my current role as Director of Strategy at Briteweb, I lead strategy for a creative agency serving nonprofits, foundations, B Corps and other purpose-driven organizations. Much of that work is about helping complex organizations turn their website and digital presence into a more effective system: attracting the right audiences, clarifying pathways, improving content and calls to action, and making it easier for people to take meaningful next steps, whether that is registering for a program, subscribing for updates, making a donation or reaching out for more information.

I also look for the repeatable system underneath the immediate problem. How do we solve this once, learn from it and make the next version easier, stronger or more effective? That can mean improving a content model, creating a better reporting rhythm, refining a digital workflow, building a campaign system or designing a process that helps a team make better decisions without starting from scratch every time.

This is where I believe I can bring real value to Arts Umbrella. The role calls for someone who can connect brand, digital presence, audience engagement, enrolment, fundraising support, storytelling, reporting and team leadership into one stronger marketing and communications function. That is the kind of work I am best suited to do.

I have also included a short note on my approach to marketing and communications leadership. I do not see it as a set of recommendations from the outside. I see it as a reflection of how I would enter the role: by learning the organization deeply, understanding the goals and data, listening to the people already doing the work and then helping the team build a stronger marketing function over time.

I would be grateful for the opportunity to bring my experience in strategy to Arts Umbrella.

Sincerely,
Thomas Gage
